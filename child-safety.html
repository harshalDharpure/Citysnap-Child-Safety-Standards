Child Safety Standards (CSAE / CSAM)

Effective date: March 31, 2026
App: Citysnap — local city thoughts, snaps & conversations
Contact: harshaldharpure9922@gmail.com

Citysnap is a local social app where people in the same city share thoughts about work, life, traffic, rent, relationships, college, and everyday city moments. Posts use your real display name. We are committed to protecting children and preventing child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) and child sexual abuse material (CSAM) on our platform.

1. Zero tolerance for CSAE/CSAM

Citysnap has zero tolerance for:
  • Any content that sexually exploits or endangers minors
  • Any CSAM (including links, imagery, grooming content, or instructions)
  • Sexual content involving minors in any form
  • Using Citysnap to contact, groom, or solicit minors

Accounts engaging in CSAE/CSAM-related activity may be removed immediately and reported as required by law.

2. Prohibited content and behavior

On Citysnap, the following are strictly prohibited in posts, comments, photos (snaps), profiles, and messages:

  • Sexual content involving anyone under 18 (real or fictional)
  • Requests for sexual content involving minors
  • Grooming, enticement, or attempts to meet minors for sexual purposes
  • Sharing, requesting, or distributing CSAM
  • Content that promotes or normalizes sexual exploitation of minors
  • Posting photos of minors in a sexualized context, or sharing a minor’s private information to harm or exploit them
  • Using the app while misrepresenting your age to interact with minors

Citysnap is intended for users 13 years of age and older. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

3. Reporting and moderation

Users can help keep Citysnap safe:

  • Report posts or comments that violate our standards (available from the post menu)
  • Block users you do not want to see

We review reports and take appropriate action, including content removal, account suspension, or permanent bans. Repeated or severe violations, especially involving minors, are prioritized.

If you believe a child is in immediate danger, contact your local emergency services first.

4. Cooperation with authorities

Citysnap complies with applicable child safety laws. Where legally required, we may preserve relevant information and report CSAE/CSAM to appropriate regional or national authorities, including law enforcement and organizations such as NCMEC where applicable.

5. Contact for child safety concerns

For CSAE/CSAM concerns, law enforcement requests, or urgent safety issues, contact:

Email: harshaldharpure9922@gmail.com
General support: support@citysnap.app

Please include:

  • The relevant post or comment (quote or description)
  • Any screenshots (if available)
  • Date/time and any usernames or post IDs visible in-app
  • Your city feed context if relevant (e.g. which city filter you were viewing)

6. Updates

We may update these standards. Changes will be reflected by updating the effective date on this page.
